Layer Stack — Core Concept

Fill Layer — flat solid material across entire mesh(layer type)
Paint Layer — hand-painted strokes on mesh(layer type)
Smart Material — pre-built complex material(layer type)
Mask (black) — hide layer(mask)
Mask (white) — show layer(mask)
Smart Mask — procedural mask from curvature / AO / position(mask type)
Generator — drives masks from mesh data (edge wear, dirt)(effect)
Anchor Points — reference a layer from another layer(effect)
Layer stack renders bottom-up, just like Photoshop. Smart Masks are the key to realistic procedural wear without hand-painting every surface.
Anchor Points let you drive a mask or effect from any other layer's output — powerful for creating linked, interdependent material effects.

Baking — Critical First Step

Open bake dialogTexture Set Settings → Bake Mesh Maps

AO — ambient occlusion map(map type)

Position — world-space gradient map(map type)
Thickness — subsurface thickness map(map type)
World Space Normal — for advanced effects(map type)
Production bake resolution4096
Draft bake resolution2048
Auto-match high / low by mesh namesuffix _high and _low
Baking MUST be done before painting if you plan to use Generators or Smart Masks — they rely entirely on baked mesh map data to drive their effects.
Set your High Definition Meshes in the bake dialog. Use the "Match by Mesh Name" option with _high / _low suffixes for clean automatic pairing of high and low poly.

Texturing a Game Asset — Full Pipeline

1
UV unwrap in Blender or Maya — non-overlapping islands, consistent texel density across the mesh
2
Export as OBJ or FBX; import into Substance Painter via File → New → load mesh
3
Bake all mesh maps (Normal, AO, Curvature, Position) at 4K resolution before any painting begins
4
Build base fill layers for each material zone — metal, fabric, plastic, glass — one fill layer per zone
5
Add Smart Materials or Generators for procedural wear: edge damage, dirt accumulation, surface scratches
6
Paint unique hand-crafted details on Paint layers — decals, text markings, scuffs, stains, unique wear patterns
7
Export with the correct preset for your render target: Unreal Engine, Unity, or Blender/Cycles
Work material zone by material zone — finish one material completely before moving to the next. This keeps the layer stack readable and manageable on complex assets.
