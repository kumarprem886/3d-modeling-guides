How holes work: Place a Hole shape inside a Solid shape → select both → press Ctrl+G to group. The hole shape is subtracted from the solid, creating a cutout. You can ungroup to edit separately.
Always select all shapes you want in the final object before grouping. Partial grouping can leave floating geometry that causes print issues.

Shape Generators are community-created parametric shapes: gears, springs, Minecraft characters, architectural elements, and more. Access them in the right-side shapes panel.
Import an SVG logo → Tinkercad automatically extrudes it into a 3D object. Great for creating custom name tags, logos, and badges for printing.
Tip: Duplicate with Ctrl+D then immediately move — this is faster than copy/paste and keeps objects organized on the workplane.
Tip: Use the Align tool constantly — dragging shapes by eye is never accurate. Select multiple shapes, click Align, then click the alignment dots for pixel-perfect positioning.
Tip: Always design at 1:1 real-world scale in mm from the very start. Tinkercad defaults to mm — don't change it. 1 unit = 1mm.
Warning: Tinkercad has no undo history beyond approximately 30 steps. Save design versions frequently by duplicating the design in your dashboard before making major changes.
Warning: Very complex models with many grouped shapes can slow down the browser significantly. Simplify by grouping intermediate stages and breaking complex designs into parts.
Info: Use Tinkercad Codeblocks for programming-based design — it uses a Scratch-style visual block interface to generate 3D shapes with loops and variables. Great intro to coding.
Info: Tinkercad Circuits is a built-in electronics and Arduino circuit simulator. Design and test circuits before buying hardware — perfect for STEM education.
Info: Group everything into a single object before exporting STL. This ensures you export a single manifold (watertight) solid that slicers can process without errors.

Workflow: First 3D Print

  1. Sign up for a free account at tinkercad.com. From your dashboard, click "Create new design" to open the editor.
  2. Drag a Box shape from the right panel onto the workplane. Click the shape and use the Ruler tool to set exact dimensions: 30mm × 30mm × 5mm.
  3. Drag another Box shape onto the workplane. In the inspector panel (top-right when selected), change it to Hole mode. Resize it to 20mm × 20mm × 10mm and position it centered on top of the first box to create a pocket.
  4. Select both shapes (click one, then Shift+click the other). Press Ctrl+G to group — the hole cuts through the solid, creating a recessed pocket.
  5. Add text: drag a Text shape onto the workplane, click it to type your name. Resize and position it on the top face of your box. Group with the main shape.
  6. Click Export (top-right menu) → select .STL. Open the STL in Cura or PrusaSlicer → set your layer height and supports → slice → send to printer!
Tinkercad is completely free with no feature limitations for personal use. It's maintained by Autodesk and regularly updated with new features.
Once you outgrow Tinkercad, the natural next step is Fusion 360 (also by Autodesk, free for personal use) which offers full parametric CAD capabilities.
