The Lychee Workflow
  1. Import — drag STL/OBJ/3MF file into the viewport or use Ctrl+I

  3. Repair (if needed) — Tools → Repair to fix mesh errors before slicing

Print Settings — Exposure & Layer Configuration
Every knob that controls how your resin cures — and exactly what to set each one to.
Exposure Settings
SettingTypical RangeNotes
Bottom (Initial) Exposure35 – 60 sFirst N layers cure much longer to bond to FEP/plate. Too short = plate adhesion failure. Too long = elephant foot.
Normal Exposure2 – 4 s (4K) / 2.5 – 5 s (8K/12K)Main layer cure time. Higher resolution screens need slightly more exposure. Calibrate with matrix test.
Bottom Layer Count4 – 8 layersHow many layers receive bottom exposure time. 6 is a safe default.
Transition Layers4 – 6 layersGradual exposure ramp-down from bottom to normal. Prevents delamination at the transition zone.
Light-off Delay0 – 2 sWait time after UV before lift begins. Allows resin to fully cure before peel force.
Rest After Retract0 – 2 sWait after platform descends before UV fires. Allows resin to settle and fill layer gap.
Rest After Lift0 – 2 sPause at top of lift before descending. Helps resin flow under platform.
Overexposure symptoms: Elephant foot (base flares out), loss of fine detail, supports fused to model, stuck to FEP. Reduce normal exposure by 0.2-0.5s increments.
Underexposure symptoms: Layers not adhering, print falls off mid-way, cloudy/soft surfaces, supports break. Increase exposure by 0.3-0.5s increments.
Lift & Retract Settings
SettingStandardNotes
Lift Height6 – 8 mmHow high the platform rises after each layer. More = better FEP peel, slower print.
Bottom Lift Height8 – 10 mmExtra peel distance for initial layers. First layers have strongest suction.
Lift Speed (up)2 – 4 mm/sSlower = less peel stress, less FEP wear. For large flat areas: 1–2 mm/s.
Retract Speed (down)3 – 6 mm/sPlatform descending. Can be faster than lift since no peel happening.
Two-Stage Lift (Highly Recommended)

Two-stage lift uses a slow initial movement to break FEP adhesion gently, then moves quickly for the rest of the lift. This dramatically reduces suction cup forces and FEP wear.

PhaseDistanceSpeed
Bottom Phase 1 (slow peel)1 – 2 mm1 mm/s
Bottom Phase 2 (fast lift)Remaining4 mm/s
Normal Phase 1 (slow peel)1 – 2 mm1 – 2 mm/s
Normal Phase 2 (fast lift)Remaining3 – 5 mm/s
FEP life tip: Two-stage lift is the single biggest factor in FEP longevity. A single-speed fast lift creates suction pop that stretches and clouds the FEP membrane much faster.

Lychee Built-In Calibration Tool

Lychee includes an exposure matrix calibration test equivalent to RERF (Resin Exposure Range Finder). Access it from: Tools → Exposure Calibration or from the Resin Profile panel.

  1. Set your starting exposure estimate (from community profile or resin bottle)
  2. Configure the matrix: number of steps and exposure increment per step (e.g., 0.2s steps, 8 steps)
  3. Lychee generates a test grid of small objects at each exposure value
  4. Slice and print the calibration file
  5. Examine results: find the exposure value that shows crisp detail without elephant foot or blurring
  6. Enter that value as your confirmed normal exposure in the resin profile
Community tests: Lychee Island also hosts community-made calibration files (AmeraLabs Town, XY calibration squares, etc.) that are more comprehensive than the built-in test.
Anti-Aliasing Settings

Anti-aliasing in resin slicers smooths the jagged pixel edges that result from the LCD screen's fixed pixel grid. Lychee offers both AA level and grey-level control.

SettingOptionsEffect
AA LevelOff / 2x / 4x / 8xHigher = smoother curved edges, slightly softer very fine detail
Grey Level0 / 1 / 2 / 3Partial pixel exposure on edges; 0 = binary (on/off), 3 = maximum softening
Figurines / organic: 4x AA + Grey Level 2 — smooth curves and natural surfaces look best
Technical parts: 2x AA + Grey Level 1 — preserve sharp corners and avoid over-softening tight tolerances

Island Detection Workflow
  1. Add supports (Smart Support or Classic Auto)
  2. Click Analyze → Check Islands (Ctrl+Shift+R)
  3. Islands appear highlighted in orange in the layer preview view
  4. Click each orange island — Lychee shows which layer number and exact position
  5. Right-click island → "Add Support Here" or switch to Support tool and click manually
  6. Re-run Analyze after adding — repeat until island count = 0
Never skip island check. A single unsupported island floating in mid-air will detach, float in the resin vat, and potentially cure against the FEP — ruining subsequent layers and your FEP membrane.
Smart Support Workflow (Paid — Step by Step)
  1. Import model and orient it for minimal flat horizontal overhangs (tilt 25–45°)
  2. Click the Smart Support button in the left toolbar
  3. Lychee analyzes full 3D geometry — takes 5–30 seconds depending on model complexity
  4. Review the placed supports visually — Smart Support avoids visible surfaces where possible
  5. Switch to Layer Preview ([/]) and check every 5–10 layers for orange islands
  6. Add manual supports for any remaining islands
  7. Compare to Chitubox on the same model — typically 40–60% fewer supports, same print success
Manual Support Tips & Tricks
Mouse Controls in Support Mode
  • Middle-click on model surface = add one support at that point
  • Ctrl + click on support = remove that support
  • Paint mode: brush adds/removes many supports by painting over surface area
  • Layer mode: switch to a specific layer in preview and click to place support exactly at that height
Anti-Suction Tips
  • Large flat horizontal overhangs: add very dense support grid (every 3–5 mm)
  • Orient model to tilt flat areas away from FEP plate
  • Lychee shows suction risk warning for sealed hollow cavities
  • When warned about suction: add drain holes + ensure orientation allows resin flow
Hollowing, Drain Holes & Scene Management
Reduce resin cost, prevent suction cup failures, and manage complex multi-plate projects efficiently.
Hollowing in Lychee
  1. Select model, then press H or click the Hollow tool in the left toolbar
  2. The hollow settings panel opens on the right
  3. Set Wall Thickness: 1.5 – 3 mm (1.5mm for decorative, 2–3mm for structural)
  4. Choose Infill type: None / Grid / Gyroid / Lattice (None = fully empty interior)
  5. Set Infill Density: 10–30% if using infill (adds internal structure without full solid mass)
  6. Check Seal top/bottom if model has open ends that need closing
  7. Click Apply, then verify with X-ray view (Ctrl+X) — you should see the hollow shell clearly
After hollowing, always add drain holes. Uncured liquid resin trapped inside a sealed hollow will continue to off-gas and may crack your print days later.
Drain Hole Placement
  1. With hollow model selected: go to Tools → Drain Holes or click the hole icon in the Hollow panel
  2. Click on the model surface to place holes (enter placement mode first)
  3. Settings: Diameter 3–5 mm, depth set to "through wall" (must penetrate completely)
  4. Lychee can auto-suggest drain hole positions based on the hollow cavity and current orientation — use this as a starting point
  5. Manually ensure: 1 hole at the lowest point of the model (resin drain out) + 1 hole at the highest point (air vent in)
  6. Switch to X-ray view — confirm each hole visibly penetrates through the shell wall entirely
Minimum holes: At least 2 holes — one low (drain), one high (vent). More holes = faster draining and less trapped resin.
Wash step: After printing, shake the model in IPA with holes facing down to flush uncured resin completely out of the interior.

Resin Temperature Management

Cold resin (<18 °C): Significantly slower cure, layer separation, failed prints. Solution: heat resin bottle in a warm water bath (NOT boiling), use a resin heater mat, or print in a warm room.
Hot resin (>30 °C): May cure too fast. Reduce exposure by 0.2–0.5 s.
Ideal range: 22–26 °C for most resins.

Resin Mixing & Custom Formulas

Toughened blend80 % ABS-Like + 20 % Tenacious
Flexible-rigid blend70 / 30 for semi-flexible parts

Color mixing: Use UV-compatible resin pigments only — do not use FDM pigments.

Always re-calibrate exposure when mixing resins. The combined cure rate differs from either component alone.

Orientation Mastery for Resin

The Physics of Resin Orientation

Each layer must peel away from the FEP film before lifting. The force required = cross-sectional area × suction pressure.

Large cross-section at any single layer = HIGH peel force = risk of support failure or print separation from plate.
Goal: minimize the largest cross-section at any one layer.

The 45° Rule

Tilt almost everything 45° (or more). This achieves four things simultaneously:

  1. Spreads the cross-section over multiple layers — reduces max force per layer.
  2. Eliminates flat horizontal surfaces — main cause of suction.
  3. Reduces support count — overhangs are shorter spans.
  4. Better surface quality on curved areas — stepping is less visible at an angle.
Exception: Some flat technical parts must remain flat where dimensional accuracy takes priority over peel force.

Orientation by Model Type

Humanoid Figures / Characters

45° back tilt + 30° side tilt. Face and chest face toward viewer. Supports land on back and base — hidden areas.

Miniatures (D&D / Warhammer Scale)

  • 45° back + 20° side for typical heroic pose.
  • Sword / weapon tips: must be supported — add manual support at tip.
  • Base of miniature: build-plate side — add raft.

Rings / Jewelry

  • 45° angle through the band's diameter.
  • Stone / gem face UP (away from supports — no scars on display surface).
  • Inner band accepts support scars — hidden area.
  • Use light supports only on inner band surface.

Flat / Coin-Like Objects (Medallions, Tokens)

NEVER flat on plate — suction disaster + elephant foot on display face.

Stand on edge at 45°. Supports along bottom edge only.

Dental / Denture Models

15–20° tilt only (accuracy needed). Supports along perimeter. Keep occlusal (bite) surface clean of support scars.

Large Rectangular Objects (Enclosures, Brackets)

45° diagonal tilt on both X and Y. Dramatically reduces any single-layer cross-section. Can also reduce print time (shorter actual Z height when tilted).

Hollow Vessels (Cups, Bowls)

Opening facing DOWN in print orientation. Liquid resin drains out instead of being trapped. Interior wall supports are hidden by the vessel interior.

Lychee Auto-Orient

Select model → Analyze → Auto-Orient. Lychee evaluates 500+ orientations and picks the lowest support count + lowest max cross-section.

Auto-Orient optimizes for structure, not cosmetics. It may support visible surfaces. Use it as a starting point, then manually adjust for display quality.

Cross-Section Check Workflow

  1. After orienting: switch to Layer Preview.
  2. Scrub through layers with [ ] keys.
  3. Note the largest (widest) layer cross-section — this is your highest-risk layer.
  4. If very large: add more supports at that height, or re-orient the model.
  5. Check for islands at every major cross-section change.

Troubleshooting Resin Prints in Lychee

Export Format Wrong

Symptom: Printer shows "file error" or won't read the file.

Fix: Verify your machine profile is set to your exact printer model. Lychee uses the wrong format if the machine profile is incorrect.

Check the file extension: .ctb for most Elegoo printers, .photon for the original Photon, etc.

Community Profile Issues

Profile too aggressive (failures)Different resin batch — add +0.5 s to exposure
Profile too conservative (over-exposure)Reduce normal exposure by 0.3–0.5 s
Old profiles for earlier firmware may not apply correctly to new firmware. Download a newer profile or create your own from a calibration print.

Island Detection False Positives

Lychee may flag "islands" that are actually connected at a layer below.

Verify: Look at the layer immediately BELOW the flagged island. If connected, it is a false positive.

A true island = completely isolated pixels on one layer, not connected to anything within 1–2 layers above or below.

Smart Support Over-Supporting

Lychee sometimes adds too many supports on clearly self-supported areas.

Fix: After Smart Support, enter remove mode — Ctrl+Click a support to delete it.

  • Safe to remove: Supports on flat surfaces with a slope >45° that would self-support.
  • Never remove: Supports in areas with <30° overhang or on isolated islands.

Lychee Performance (Slow on Complex Models)

Symptom: Large STLs (over 10 MB) cause slow auto-support or slow slice preview.

Fix: Decimate the mesh in Blender or Meshmixer first — reduce polygon count without losing visible detail.

Alternatively: use a lower AA setting while setting up the scene, then increase AA to maximum before final export.

Hollow Model Issues

Hollow doesn't apply to all partsModel has disconnected shells — repair mesh first
Drain hole not cutting throughIncrease depth so it exceeds wall thickness
X-ray shows cavity not fully closedEnable "seal inner holes" in hollow settings

Practice Projects — 6 Lychee-Specific Challenges

Project 1 — Lychee Calibration Square Test

Goal: Find optimal exposure using Lychee's built-in calibration system.

  1. Open Lychee → File → New → Calibration Test (or import from Lychee Island).
  2. Set starting exposure: community profile suggestion minus 0.5 s.
  3. Lychee calibration test prints 4–5 sections at +0.5 s increments.
  4. Print, wash, cure, examine.
  5. Look for: sharpest thin pins, clean lettering separation, no surface blobs.
  6. Enter winning exposure into your resin profile in Lychee.
  7. Save profile with a descriptive name: "Elegoo Saturn3 + ABS-Like2.0 @ 2.2s"
  8. Upload to Lychee Island to help others with the same setup.
Skills: Lychee calibration system, profile creation, community sharing.

Project 2 — Miniature Figure (Smart Support Showcase)

Goal: Print a 32mm scale miniature with minimal support scars on detail surfaces.

  1. Download a 32mm miniature STL (MyMiniFactory, Lychee Island, Printables). Scale to 32mm height if needed.
  2. Orient: 45° back tilt, 20° side tilt. Face pointing toward viewer.
  3. Ctrl+Shift+A → Smart Support (wait for analysis).
  4. Review in layer mode → fix any islands.
  5. Manually add support to: sword / weapon tips, small protrusions, ear tips.
  6. Manually remove supports from: face, chest, visible equipment surfaces.
  7. Hollow if >40mm: 1.5mm wall, no infill, 2× 3mm drain holes at lowest points.
  8. Layer height: 0.03 mm (30µm) for best miniature detail.
  9. Print, wash gently with soft brush in IPA, cure 5 minutes each side.
  10. Remove supports. Examine detail vs. support scars.
Skills: Smart Support, manual support editing, miniature-specific orientation.

Project 3 — Articulated Print (Print-in-Place Assembly)

Goal: Print a print-in-place dragon or snake with articulated joints in a single run.

  1. Download a print-in-place articulated dragon / fish / lizard from Printables or Lychee Island. These models have gaps between segments (~0.3mm tolerance).
  2. Orient: flat orientation often works (joints parallel to plate).
  3. Supports: none under gaps / joints — supports in joints = fused joints. Only support outer extremities (head, tail tip) if needed.
  4. Layer height: 0.05 mm. Exposure: normal (not over — over-exposure fuses joints).
  5. Island check: each joint ring / segment should appear connected even with gaps.
  6. Print. Wash holding upside down so IPA flows through joints.
  7. Before curing: flex the model gently to break any surface tension between joints.
  8. Cure: hang from tail so all surfaces receive UV.
Skills: Tolerance printing, joint design, pre-cure part manipulation.

Project 4 — Pre-Supported Commercial File

Goal: Learn to use designer-provided pre-supported files and analyze professional support strategies.

  1. Purchase or download a pre-supported resin STL from Lychee Island, MyMiniFactory, or Patreon.
  2. Import into Lychee — the file arrives with supports already modeled into the STL.
  3. Do NOT auto-orient — already set for the intended orientation.
  4. Do NOT add your own supports — duplicates the designer's work.
  5. Set your calibrated exposure profile for your resin.
  6. Slice → preview → check for islands (should be none — the designer handled them).
  7. Export and print.
  8. Evaluate support removal ease vs. auto-supports.
  9. Analyze the designer's choices: why those support positions?
  10. Apply the lessons to your own unsupported model orientations.
Skills: Pre-supported files, learning from professionals, critical evaluation.

Project 5 — Large Terrain Piece / Diorama Base

Goal: Print a large scenic base with efficient hollowing and drainage planning.

  1. Download a terrain base or diorama scene (~150×100mm, complex surface).
  2. Orient: slight 15° tilt to avoid suction on the flat base.
  3. Hollow: 2mm walls, Gyroid infill at 15% for rigidity without solid fill.
  4. Drain holes: 3–4 holes across the base + 2 vent holes (resin can pool in cavities).
  5. Supports: dense support grid on the underside — large prints need solid anchoring.
  6. Verify cross-section: walk through layers — each should be a manageable area.
  7. Lift speed: slow (1.5–2mm/s) — large cross-sections need slow peel.
  8. Print time: likely 8–16 hours — run overnight.
  9. After print: drain resin from holes while still over the resin tray.
  10. Wash in ultrasonic cleaner or agitate IPA to reach all crevices.
  11. Cure in sections with UV torch if the piece doesn't fit the curing station.
Skills: Large print management, complex hollowing, drainage planning.

Project 6 — Lychee Island Collaboration (Advanced)

Goal: Contribute to and use the Lychee community ecosystem.

  1. Find a printer + resin combination on Lychee Island with few or poor profiles.
  2. Run a calibration test (Project 1 workflow) and document results carefully.
  3. Print 3–4 test models to verify the profile is reliable.
  4. In Lychee: export your profile.
  5. Upload to Lychee Island: add description, exposure settings, and notes on resin behavior.
  6. After 2 weeks: check if others used your profile and left feedback.
  7. Iterate: if feedback shows failures, adjust and update the profile.
  8. Browse Lychee Island for models: filter by "pre-supported" and download 5 different model types.
  9. Compare pre-supported approaches across different designers.
  10. Build your library: organize downloaded profiles, models, and calibration records.
Skills: Community engagement, profile management, giving back to the maker community.

Expert Tips & Mastering Resin Printing

Pro Workflow Tips

  • Save projects as .lys (Lychee native) — preserves everything including support placement and scene settings.
  • Use version naming: dragon_v1_unsupported.lys → dragon_v2_supported.lys → dragon_v3_printed.lys
  • Template scenes: create a template.lys with your calibrated machine + resin settings pre-loaded — import new models into the template.
  • Batch similar models: fill the plate with similar-sized models using the same settings rather than printing individually.
  • Resin estimation: Lychee shows exact mL needed — plan pours accordingly. Never pour a full bottle into the tank.
