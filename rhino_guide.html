Grasshopper is Rhino's built-in parametric visual programming plugin. Wire together nodes to build complex generative geometry driven by sliders and equations — no traditional coding required.
Data Trees in Grasshopper = lists of geometry, with branches as groupings. Understanding data trees is the key to unlocking Grasshopper's full power for complex patterns and arrays.
Grasshopper supports C# and Python scripting nodes for custom logic beyond the built-in nodes. This enables full algorithmic design workflows.

Workflow: Industrial Design

  1. Create concept curves (profiles, rails, cross-sections) by tracing over imported reference images placed as picture frames in the viewport.
  2. Build primary surfaces using Sweep2, Loft, or NetworkSrf — choose based on how many rails and profiles define your shape.
  3. Match surface continuity with MatchSrf: aim for G1 (tangent) minimum, G2 (curvature) for premium products. Check with Zebra or curvature analysis.
  4. Cap open surfaces with Cap, boolean join into one solid with BooleanUnion, then run Check to verify geometry is valid and watertight.
  5. Apply FilletEdge on all hard edges — the radius defines the product's character (tight for tech, large for consumer goods).
  6. Export to Keyshot for photorealistic rendering (OBJ or 3DM format), or export as STEP to Fusion 360 for manufacturing preparation.
Rhino is a paid software (one-time purchase, ~$995 educational, ~$995-$2000 commercial). It does not have a free tier, but a 90-day full-featured trial is available.
Rhino is the industry standard for product design, jewelry, architecture, and marine design — anywhere complex freeform NURBS surfaces are required.
