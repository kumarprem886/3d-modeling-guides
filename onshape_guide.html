Onshape's version control means you can never lose your work. Every change is tracked and you can restore any previous state at any time — no more "save as V1, V2, V3_final_FINAL" file naming.
Branching lets teams work on design variations in parallel without overwriting each other's work — then merge the best elements back into the main design.

Workflow: Team Collaboration

  1. Create a free account at onshape.com. Click Create New Document from your dashboard. Name your document descriptively.
  2. Add a Part Studio tab. Set your units (millimeters for metric). Create a sketch on the origin plane — click the plane in the graphics area.
  3. Model your part parametrically: Sketch a profile → E to Extrude → add more sketches on faces for cuts and features → add Fillets on edges.
  4. Add an Assembly tab. Insert your parts. Fix the first part. Add mates (Fastened, Revolute, etc.) to define how parts move relative to each other.
  5. Share the document with teammates using the top-right Share button. Set their permission (view-only or can edit). They open the link in their own browser and see your changes in real time.
  6. Add a Drawing tab: Insert model views → add projected views (front, top, right, isometric) → apply Smart Dimensions and annotations.
  7. Export: STEP for manufacturing / CNC, STL for 3D printing, PDF of the drawing for the shop floor. All exports from the top File menu.
Onshape is free for public documents and students. The Professional plan (paid) adds private documents, custom properties, and enterprise features.
On the free plan, all documents are public — anyone can find and view them. Do not use the free plan for proprietary or confidential designs. Use the Education or Professional plan for private work.
Onshape runs natively on iOS and Android tablets and phones — you can view, annotate, and even model on your phone in the field. The mobile app is free with your account.
